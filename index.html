Types of meteorites and asteroids

Stony Meteorite

They are the most common type (90-95% of meteorites). Composed of silicate minerals similar to Earth’s rocks.

About the Asteroid Impactor

Around 66 million years ago, a giant asteroid known as the Chicxulub Impactor struck Earth near today’s Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico. The asteroid, estimated to be about 10 km in diameter, created a massive crater and caused dramatic climate changes. Scientists believe this impact played a major role in the extinction of the dinosaurs and reshaped life on our planet.

Importance of Monitoring

- Risk Assessment: Monitoring helps assess the potential risks of asteroid and meteoroid impacts on Earth.
- Preparedness and Response: It enables scientists and engineers to develop strategies to respond to asteroids and meteoroids that pose a threat to Earth.

Continuous monitoring of asteroids and meteoroids is essential to ensure the safety and protection of Earth from potential space threats.

Monitoring asteroids and meteoroids is a critical component of planetary defense and helps safeguard our planet from potential hazards.
